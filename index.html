Litigation status: Reflects case outcomes and posture as of March 18, 2026. The legal landscape is extremely volatile; multiple cases have reversed within months. Always verify current status against court records.

Age threshold: Reflects the threshold for the age assurance/verification trigger. Some laws have additional provisions at different age cutoffs (e.g., Florida restricts accounts for under-16 while applying data obligations for under-18).

Mechanism types: "Government ID" = requires digitized or scanned government-issued photo ID; "Commercially reasonable" = flexible standard allowing multiple methods; "Age estimation" = uses behavioral, biometric, or other signals to infer age without requiring ID; "Audience-based" = obligations triggered by audience composition assessment rather than individual user verification.

Excluded: State laws restricted to offline venues; COPPA Rule proposed rulemaking details; laws requiring age assurance only for alcohol/tobacco/gambling purchases. UK Online Safety Act and Ofcom guidance covered separately.
